About me

I am an associate professor in computer science at Bordeaux University and LaBRI, member of the teams Logical foundations of computing and Synthesis.

My research interest is mainly in algorithms and computational complexity.

Office Hours – Let’s talk!

I’m starting a new experiment: a weekly 1-hour open discussion slot for anyone—colleagues, students, or anyone curious.

There’s no fixed agenda. We can discuss all sorts of things: research and academic questions, teaching organization and planning, career paths and mentoring, interdisciplinary ideas, your latest wild idea… or just have an informal chat.

Meetings can happen in person at LaBRI, in a coffee shop, online—wherever works best. Let’s connect and see where the conversation takes us.

Background

Before moving to Bordeaux, I was a research engineer at Criteo (2021–2023), working on large-scale recommendation systems. Previously, I was a postdoc in the DeepSynth CNRS Momentum project (2019–2021) with Nathanaël Fijalkow, and before that, in the Approximation and Proof Complexity group with Jakob Nordström, Johan Håstad, and Per Austrin. I defended my PhD in 2018 at IRIF, University Paris Diderot, under the supervision of Sophie Laplante and Sylvain Perifel.
